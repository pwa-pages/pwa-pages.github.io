(Very) quick antichess guide

New to antichess?. Here are some small tips for starters (click on a row to see an example):

  • Keep your king

    this is not a hard rule but very useful in many situations.

  • Have your pieces control squares on the board

    this gives you more choice and opponent less which is key in antichess.

  • Don't give away strong pieces

    Don't give away strong pieces without good reason (king, rook, queen).

  • Learn about intermediates

    (see links below, youtube or lichess study, or click the row).

  • Watch your queen

    Queen is strong in opening, but dangerous to have going to endgame.

  • Watch your knights

    Knights are in general hard to play with in endgame.

  • Watch your rooks

    Rooks are in general very good to have in endgame.

  • Watch your bishops

    Bishops are very dangerous in opening, many forced opening lines are with bishop cleaning up the board.

  • Losing lines

    d3, d4, e4 are instantly losing for white.

  • Opening lines

    Find some opening lines with white or black that are not instantly losing (use analysis engine on lichess or check strong players opening repertoire).

  • Play in tournaments

    Play in lichess arena tournaments, there are often a diverse range of players which is beneficial for improving.

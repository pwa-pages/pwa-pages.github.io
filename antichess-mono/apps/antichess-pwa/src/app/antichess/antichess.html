Rules

The rules of this game are the same as for normal Chess (starting position and piece movement) with the following differences:

  • - No castling is allowed.
  • - A pawn can promote to king.
  • - There is no check or checkmate.
  • - Who loses all pieces including the king or cannot make a legal move, wins the game.
  • - Capturing of the opponent's pieces is mandatory — a player must capture if any capture is available; only if no capture is possible may they make a non‑capturing move.


(Very) quick antichess guide

New to antichess?. Here are some small tips for starters:

  • - Keep your king, this is not a hard rule but very useful in many situations.
  • - Queen is strong in opening, but dangerous to have going to endgame.
  • - Knights are in general hard to play with in endgame.
  • - Rooks are in general very good to have in endgame.
  • - Bishops are very dangerous in opening, many forced opening lines are with bishop cleaning up the board.
  • - d3, d4, e4 are instantly losing for white.
  • - Find some opening lines with white or black that are not instantly losing (use analysis engine on lichess or check strong players opening repetoire)
  • - Play in lichess arena tournaments, there are often a diverse range of players which is beneficial for improving.
